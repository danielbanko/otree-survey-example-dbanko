  1. Computer tells you the value of X.
    • The value of X may be different each time you play the game.
  2. Computer fills the buckets.
    • The two buckets are filled with five balls each.
    • Bucket A has 4 light balls and 1 dark ball.
    • Bucket B has 1 light ball and 4 dark balls.
    • You will always see the exact number of light and dark balls in the two urns.
  3. Computer selects Mystery Bucket.
    • There is an X-in-10 chance that Bucket A is selected as the Mystery Bucket.
  4. You report your first Belief that the Mystery Bucket is Bucket A.
    • Provide your best guess about the chance that the computer selected Bucket A knowing X and before seeing the ball.
  5. Computer shows you a randomly selected ball from the Mystery Bucket.
    • Each ball in the Mystery Bucket is equally likely to be selected.
  6. You report your second Belief that the Mystery Bucket is Bucket A.
    • Provide your best guess about the chance that the computer selected Bucket A knowing X after seeing the ball.
